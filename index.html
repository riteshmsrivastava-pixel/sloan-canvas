How to use Sloan Canvas

Plan your Fall 2026 electives and bids in a few minutes.
1

Browse & filter the catalog (left)

Narrow ~70 Fall courses by career track, certificate, route (Bid / Apply / Register) or search. Keep “only courses I’m eligible to bid for” on to hide undergrad/MFIN/repeat-core sections.

2

Build your plan

Tap + (or drag a card) to add a course to My Fall Plan on the right. Tap − to remove it. Your core 15.414 is already locked in.

3

Enter your bids

For biddable Sloan courses, type points in the Bid box. You have 1,000 points — and you pay the clearing price, not your bid. Tiers (High/Medium/Low) are rough guidance.

4

Watch the limits

The bars track points /1000, elective units /57 and total units /66. The header shows what’s remaining, live.

5

Check your week

The calendar plots every course. A red outline = time clash. Half-term courses (H1 vs H2) don’t clash with each other.

6

Use the hints

Green 🔁 Also offered means you could take it in Spring/IAP instead — don’t overspend now. Blue ≈ Similar is a close substitute; click it to jump.

7

It’s private

Your plan saves only in this browser — nothing is shared or uploaded. Always confirm everything on SloanBid before you actually bid.

Notes for Sloan Fellows. You have 1,000 points and up to 57 elective units in Round IB (max 66 total Fall units incl. the 9-unit core 15.414 Financial Management, which is auto-assigned — not bid). Half-term courses (H1/H2) only conflict within the same half. You pay the clearing price, not your bid; bid tiers shown (High ≈ 300–700+, Medium ≈ 75–300, Low ≈ token) are directional — check SloanBid’s closed-class history.
Not biddable by SFMBA / excluded: 15.402 & 15.516 (repeat the core), 15.761 (repeats Summer core), 15.871 / 15.873 (Fall — you take System Dynamics core in Spring H3), plus undergrad-, doctoral-, MFIN- and MBA-2nd-year-restricted sections. Apply/Permission courses (G-Lab, A-Lab, Climate & Energy Ventures, CATAPULT, 15.739) and Register (WebSIS) courses don’t use points — calendar the deadlines.
